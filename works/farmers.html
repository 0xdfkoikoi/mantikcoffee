Farmer Education Program

Di mantik coffee kami sangat menjunjung tinggi sustainability terhadap lingkungan, kami percaya proses alami adalah proses terbaik dalam menciptakan coffee yang berkualitas baik. kondisi tanah yang baik menciptakan coffee berkualitas tinggi dengan cipta rasa yang khas selain dari processing itu sendiri. kami bekerja sama dengan petani untuk sustainability ini, selain dari memberikan edukasi kepada para petani kami juga memberikan kesempatan bagi para petani yang ingin mendalami lebih lanjut tentang semua prosses coffee di mulai dari penanaman hingga di akhir seorang barista menyajikan coffee. hal ini membuat para petani sangat bersemangat dalam mengolah kopi mereka dengan baik dan benar dan tentu menjaga sustainability di kebun milik mereka.

Challenge

Coffee Cherry Recycling

At Mantik Coffee, we see every part of the coffee plant as a gift — even the cherry skins that are often thrown away. After harvesting, most producers discard these red coffee cherries as waste. But for us, they hold new life. We collect and process the leftover cherry skins into natural compost, rich in organic nutrients that help restore soil health. This compost returns to the farms, closing the circle of sustainability. Instead of polluting the environment, the coffee’s own byproduct nourishes the next generation of trees — creating a self-sustaining ecosystem where nothing is wasted, and everything gives back. It’s a small cycle that makes a big difference — keeping the soil fertile, supporting cleaner farming, and helping our farmers grow better coffee, naturally.

Results

A final rich soil

Decomposition Stage (Initial Breakdown): Microbial Feast: As a soft, high-sugar fruit scrap, cherry skin is quickly consumed by bacteria and fungi—the primary decomposers in your compost pile. Heat Generation: This rapid microbial activity generates heat, which is essential for "hot composting." The heat helps kill weed seeds and pathogens. Moisture: Cherry skin, like most fruit, has a high water content, contributing moisture to the pile. Curing/Maturing Stage: Carbon Balance: The carbon-rich "brown" materials (like dried leaves, wood chips, or shredded paper) in your pile will break down alongside the cherry skin. The microbes use the carbon for energy and the nitrogen (from the skins) for growth. Humus Formation: Over time (months), the temperature cools, and the remaining material is broken down further by smaller organisms like worms and actinomycetes until it turns into humus (finished compost)—a dark, earthy, crumbly material.
